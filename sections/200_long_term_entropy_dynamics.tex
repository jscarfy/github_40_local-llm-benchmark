\subsection{Long-Term Dynamics in Entropy Systems}
This section investigates the long-term effects of entropy-based perturbations on the behavior of bifurcation systems.

\begin{definition}[Long-term entropy dynamics]
Long-term entropy dynamics examine how prolonged entropy perturbations affect system stability and generate periodic shifts:
\[
\| \mathcal{H}_\infty(T') - \mathcal{H}_\infty(T) \| \to 0 \quad \text{as} \quad t \to \infty.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Long-term system stability]
As entropy-driven perturbations persist, the system tends towards a stable limit set:
\[
\| \mathcal{H}_\infty(T') - \mathcal{H}_\infty(T) \| \le \epsilon, \quad \text{for small } \epsilon.
\]
\end{theorem}
